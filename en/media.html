Violas Platform Roadmap #1 Retrospective

The Violas Platform Roadmap #1 was created to include the fundamental features of the Violas platform which is built with a custom deployment on top of the Libra testnet. It was a great challenge to hit our targets and we are grateful that the goals laid out in Roadmap #1 were accomplished on schedule. Those accomplishments are as follows:

  • 1. Custom deployment of the Violas platform on top of the Libra testnet
  • 2. Adapted to the frequent Libra testnet code changes that the Violas platform is built on
  • 3. The release of bitcoin_core version 0.19.1
  • 4. Built a Libra compatible wallet from scratch (Calibra Wallet is not yet open source)
  • 5. Issued Libra compatible stablecoins that are not supported by the Libra testnet
  • 6. Developed a Proof-of-Proof protocol on the Bitcoin Blockchain
  • 7. Integrated the cross-chain functionalities between Bitcoin and Libra
  • 8. Integrated the Violas governing policy into the Violas Wallet
  • 9. Developed an SDK that can be easily integrated with existing web and mobile Apps,
  • 10. Built the appropriate regulatory requirements into the Libra compatible stablecoins offering and the Smart Stablecoin Offering (SSO) wallet
  • 11. Took the DeFi protocol (ie. DEX) developed on Ethereum and applied it to MOVE
  • 12. Built a browser for Bitcoin, Libra, and Violas, including Libra compatible stablecoins

Building the Violas platform comes with many challenges. There are the typical technical challenges that any ambitious project faces, but in our case, there is also our reliance on the unpredictable development schedule of both the Libra testnet and the Bitcoin mainnet. One particular challenge is the Libra Blockchain technology itself. Certain technologies used by Libra Blockchain are still in alpha versions and thus are not supported by other third-party software components. For example, we tried to create a Libra client on Windows to support our Windows wallet but were unable to because of these limitations. The hash algorithm used by Libra, SHA3-256, requires OpenSSL 1.1.1 version (TLSv1.3) and its support in Python 3.7.x is still in a nascent stage.

The goal of Violas Platform #2 is to add the following extra features to the Violas platform:

  • Tox, a fully encrypted messaging app from the open source community
  • Creative integration with existing messaging apps (see Note 1)
  • Easy-to-integrate SDK with popular sharing economy Apps (see Note 2)
  • Easy-to-integrate SDK with popular money transfer apps or services (see Note 3)
  • Easy-to-integrate SDK with popular payment apps (see Note 4)
  • Hosting a Validator Node for non-technical members of the Violas Association
  • White-label Violaspay wallet for third party vendors
  • White-label Violassso wallet for third party vendors
  • White-label Violasgov wallet for third party vendors
  • White-label templates for the third-party vendors

We are actively soliciting feedback from the Violas community for suggestions regarding the ease of use and ease of integration of the Violas platform with existing third-party vendors. Please submit suggestions on the following topics to violas.team@violas.io

  • Creative integration with a messaging App without authorization from the messaging App vendor
  • Simple SDK or API integration with sharing economy apps
  • Simple SDK or API integration with money transfer apps or services
  • Simple SDK or API integration with payment apps or services
  • Simple SDK or API direct integration with current Libra compatible stablecoins
  • Simple SDK or API integration with current Libra compatible stablecoins through an exchange (centralized or decentralized) such as www.coinbase.com.

We are also actively soliciting white-label partners from the global community to integrate Violas’ templates with existing third-party vendors. If interested, contact violas.team@violas.io regarding the following topics.

  • Local messaging app vendors to add Libra compatible stablecoins for their local communities
  • Sharing economy apps for ride sharing, food delivery, apartment and home rental, bike sharing, office sharing, etc. to add a Libra compatible stablecoin payment option
  • Money transfer apps or services for cross-border money transfer with exchange from one currency to another currency that uses a Libra compatible stablecoin exchange and transfer option
  • Payment apps or services for peer-to-peer direct payment without a trusted third-party
  • Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDC) for developing countries/areas, especially smaller countries/areas
  • Exchange of Libra compatible stablecoins

Definition of Terms Used: A Libra compatible stablecoin is a digital currency that is issued under similar criteria as Libra. This means that the stablecoin requires that the issuer hold an equal amount of fiat currency in a reserve to back the coin at a 1:1 ratio. It also requires that the issuer possess the proper regulatory licenses that are required by their local regulatory agency or agencies.
